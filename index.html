The word "yes" in English originates from the Old English word "gēse", which itself is likely a combination of "gēa" (meaning "so") and "sī" (meaning "be it"). This suggests that "yes" initially meant something akin to "so be it" or "truly". The word evolved through Middle English as "yes" and "yis" before becoming the modern "yes"!
